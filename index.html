Resume and Job Search • Job Platforms and Job Listings

This homework practices Class 11 content: online job-search platforms, job-listing vocabulary, and expressing job preferences.

1. Reading

Searching for the Right Job

Nina is looking for a new job online. She usually checks out Indeed and LinkedIn because they have many listings. This week, she found a hybrid administrative job that looks interesting.

She sent in her resume yesterday, but she knows that an Applicant Tracking System may parse her application before a real person reads it. She is also open to contract work, although she prefers a full-time position with benefits.

Last month, Nina had to turn down one job offer because the pay was too low. She still hopes to get into the marketing field, so she keeps applying for positions that match her goals.

2. Vocabulary

Match the job-search verb or phrase to its meaning.

Real-life English tip: When you talk about jobs in English, it sounds more natural to say I applied for a job, I sent in my resume, or I checked out the listing.

3. Writing

Write 6–8 sentences about a job posting that interests you. Start by naming the position and website, then explain why it attracts you.
